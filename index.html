About Me

The Intersection of Hardware & Intelligence

I am a Mechanical Engineer and Applied Data Scientist driven by a single goal: bridging the gap between physical hardware and intelligent software to improve human health. Currently a Researcher at the University of Florida and AI/ML Engineer for Raytheon, navigating the complex intersection of cybersecurity, generative AI, and legacy systems.

My Journey

My passion lies in medical technology. Witnessing the impact of mobility challenges on loved ones inspired me to explore how engineering can restore function and dignity. This led me to work on gait analysis for stroke patients at UF Orthopedics and to investigate advanced nanocomposites for lighter, stronger prosthetics.

I believe the next generation of medical devices won't just be mechanical — they will be intelligent. By combining my background in rigorous hardware R&D (Solar Racing, Materials Science) with advanced Data Science (LLMs, Computer Vision), I aim to build systems that are not only robust but capable of adapting to the human body.
